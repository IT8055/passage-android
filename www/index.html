ℹ️ About Passage

Passage uses the clinically validated Bristol Stool Form Scale to help you track bowel health accurately. All data is stored locally on your device — nothing is ever transmitted externally. Export your CSV and share it directly with your gastroenterologist, GP or dietitian.

⚕️ This app is a tracking tool only and does not provide medical advice. Always consult your healthcare provider about symptoms.

📋

How to use Passage

Everything you need to know about logging your gut health and sharing data with your doctor.

🚀

Getting Started

▼
1

Tap the green ➕ button at the bottom centre of the screen to open the logging form.

2

Choose your Bristol Stool Type (1–7). This is the only required field — everything else is optional but helpful.

3

Fill in as many details as you like — colour, urgency, pain, symptoms, and notes. The more you log, the more useful your data is for your doctor.

4

Tap Save Entry. Your entry is stored privately on your phone — nothing is sent anywhere.

5

Before a doctor's appointment, go to Export & Settings (⚙️) and tap Export as CSV to create a file you can share.

The Bristol Stool Scale

▼

The Bristol Stool Form Scale is a medical diagnostic tool developed at the University of Bristol in 1997. It classifies stool into 7 types and is widely used by gastroenterologists and GPs to assess bowel health.

Types 1–2 suggest constipation. Types 3–4 are considered healthy. Types 5–7 suggest increasing diarrhoea. Persistent extremes should always be discussed with your doctor.

Understanding the Other Fields

▼

🚨 Urgency (1–10)

How urgently did you need to go? 1 = no rush at all, 10 = you couldn't wait another second. High urgency is a common and significant symptom in IBD and IBS.

⚡ Pain / Discomfort (0–10)

Rate any pain during or after the movement. 0 = completely painless, 10 = severe pain. This helps your doctor see patterns of pain over time.

😖 Ease of Passing

How easy was it? This combines Bristol type with effort required. Straining even for soft stools can be a useful symptom for your doctor to know about.

🩸 Symptoms

Tap any that apply. Blood or Mucus are particularly important to log — your doctor will want to know the frequency of these. Incomplete Emptying (tenesmus) is a common IBD symptom.

📷 Photos

Completely optional. Photos are stored only on your device and are never uploaded. They can help your doctor assess consistency or colour more accurately. Photos are not included in CSV exports.

Your Data & Privacy

▼
🛡

All data is stored only on your device. Nothing is sent to any server, cloud, or third party — ever.

💾

Use Backup Data (JSON) regularly to save a copy of all your entries. If you switch devices, or uninstall and reinstall the app, you can restore from this backup.

📊

The CSV export contains all your text entries in a format your doctor can open in Excel or any spreadsheet tool. Photos are stored separately and are not included in the CSV.

🗑

Your data stays until you delete it. Uninstalling the app or using Clear All Data will permanently remove your entries, so keep a JSON backup if you want to be safe.

❓

Frequently Asked Questions

▼
Will my data be lost if I uninstall the app?
Yes — uninstalling the app (or using Clear All Data) deletes your entries. Always use the JSON backup feature (Export & Settings → Backup Data) to keep a safe copy. Restore it any time using Restore Backup.
Can I use this on multiple devices?
Not automatically — data lives on each device separately. You can transfer by exporting a JSON backup on one device and importing it on another. Start with the device you use most often.
How do I share data with my doctor?
Go to Export & Settings → Export as CSV. This creates a spreadsheet file you can share straight to email, save to your files, or print and bring to an appointment.
What if my storage is running low?
Photos use the most storage. If you notice issues, try limiting photos or removing older ones by editing entries. A JSON backup takes very little space compared to photos.
Does this replace medical advice?
No. Passage is a tracking tool to help you log symptoms and share data with your healthcare team. It does not diagnose conditions or replace professional medical advice. Always contact your doctor about concerning symptoms.
